BSc in Shipping

Department of Shipping · Faculty of Management and Economics · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The BSc in Shipping is a four-year undergraduate programme whose curriculum focuses specifically on the maritime sector, offering a degree oriented entirely toward shipping. It combines the analytical and synthetic capabilities students need with specialised scientific knowledge of the industry. The programme aims to develop students' capacity to analyse and evaluate matters related to international seaborne trade operations, maritime markets, capital management, shipping risk management, and transport and supply chain management.

The programme is designed to equip graduates with both the theoretical and practical knowledge required for a successful career in the dynamic maritime sector, or for advanced study and research. The Department describes the programme as unique, innovative in design, high in quality, and the only such degree offered by a public university in Cyprus.

Programme Description

The curriculum is structured across eight semesters, progressing from foundational courses in economics, mathematics, statistics, and English for shipping in the early years, through to specialised maritime subjects such as ship management, chartering, shipping finance, maritime law, port operations, supply chain management, and shipping risk management in the later years. Sustainability and environmental management are emphasised throughout the programme. In the final year, students may undertake an optional dissertation and/or an optional internship, and choose from specialisation and free elective courses.

Intended Learning Outcomes

Knowledge

  • Understand the fundamental principles, structures, and players of the international shipping industry, including cargo types, vessel classifications, seaborne trade routes, and supply chain integration.
  • Explain the economics of maritime markets, including the four shipping markets, supply and demand forces, freight rate cyclicality, and ship price volatility.
  • Describe the international legal and regulatory framework governing shipping, including UNCLOS, IMO and ILO conventions (SOLAS, MARPOL, STCW, MLC, ISPS) and their connection to sustainable development.
  • Understand the principles of shipping finance, shipping investment, marine insurance, chartering, port operations, and transport and supply chain management.
  • Understand sustainability principles and environmental management as they apply to the improvement of the maritime sector.

Skills

  • Apply quantitative methods — mathematics, statistics, mathematical programming, optimisation, and data analysis — to maritime problems.
  • Analyse issues connected to international seaborne trade operations and maritime markets.
  • Evaluate shipping investments using methods such as discounted cash flow, payback period, internal rate of return, and real options analysis.
  • Assess and manage market, credit, and financial risks in maritime markets using statistical methods and shipping derivatives.
  • Communicate effectively in specialised shipping English, including correspondence, safety reports, and chartering documents.

Competences

  • Manage capital and maritime risks in a professional shipping context.
  • Handle transport and supply chain management responsibilities.
  • Demonstrate critical thinking and administrative capability in addressing maritime challenges.
  • Conduct independent research applying appropriate qualitative and quantitative methodologies (developed through Research Methods courses and the optional dissertation).
  • Act with environmental awareness and a commitment to the sustainable development of the maritime sector.

  • Mathematics (Advanced Level)

Elective Subjects (2 required, chosen from the following)

  • Accounting
  • Physics

How You Will Learn

The programme combines analytical and synthetic training with specialised scientific knowledge of the maritime sector. Across the four years, students progress from foundational courses in economics, mathematics, statistics, and English for shipping, through to applied and specialised maritime subjects, supported by research-methods training in the third year.

Teaching and Learning Approach

  • Quantitative training delivered through mathematics, statistics, mathematical programming, optimisation, and data-analysis courses, using software tools such as SPSS.
  • Applied learning through case studies and industry-relevant topics in specialised maritime courses (chartering, finance, investments, risk, port operations, supply chain).
  • Research training through Research Methods in Shipping I and II, culminating in an optional dissertation in the final year.
  • Practical experience through an optional internship that provides exposure to real working conditions in the maritime sector.
  • Specialised English-for-shipping courses that develop professional communication, including correspondence, safety reports, and chartering documents.

Professional Recognition and Career Prospects

ICS Exemptions

The Institute of Chartered Shipbrokers (ICS) grants graduates of the programme the maximum number of exemptions — three exemptions — from its professional qualifying examination subjects:

  • Exemption from "Introduction to Shipping"
  • Exemption from "Economics of Sea Transport and International Trade"
  • Exemption from "Shipping Finance" OR "Ship Operations and Management" OR "Port and Terminal Management"

These three exemptions apply from 2024 onward for the new undergraduate programme, alongside the new MSc in Shipping and the interdepartmental MSc in Shipping and Finance.

Typical Job Roles and Sectors

The programme equips graduates with the theoretical and practical knowledge required for successful careers in the dynamic maritime sector, or for advanced study and research. Graduates are prepared for roles across international seaborne trade, maritime markets, capital and risk management, and transport and supply chain management.

Employment Statistics

  • 90.3% of shipping graduates are absorbed into the job market.
  • 29% gain immediate employment.
  • 50% are employed within 1–6 months.
  • 50% of undergraduate alumni pursued postgraduate studies.
