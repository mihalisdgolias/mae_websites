Bachelor in Management: Entrepreneurship and Digital Enterprise

Department of Management, Entrepreneurship and Digital Enterprise · School of Tourism Management, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The Bachelor in Management: Entrepreneurship and Digital Enterprise is a four-year undergraduate programme that gives students a solid academic foundation in business administration, marketing, accounting, human resources, and financial management, combined with specialised study in entrepreneurship, innovation, and digital business. The programme prepares graduates to create and grow new ventures, to lead the digital transformation of established organisations, and to operate effectively in both domestic and international environments while balancing corporate objectives with contemporary social and environmental needs.

The programme is built around a strong common core of general business education, complemented by two specialisation tracks. Students choose a track that aligns with their career goals while benefiting from shared foundations in economics, accounting, finance, marketing, operations, and management.

Specialisations

1. Entrepreneurship and Innovation — Focused on inventing and implementing innovative ideas, designing new business models, creating and financing new ventures, and growing startups and SMEs in domestic and international markets. Courses cover innovation management, new business model and new business creation, entrepreneurial finance, startup and SME development, international entrepreneurship, and social entrepreneurship. Prepares graduates for roles in venture creation, innovation management, and enterprise development.

2. Digital Enterprise — Focused on leveraging digital technologies to transform existing businesses and to build digital ventures. Courses cover business systems in the digital age, consumer behaviour in the digital age, basic programming principles, digital marketing and analytics, artificial intelligence and business analytics, the design and digitisation of business processes, and digital product development. Prepares graduates for digital transformation, digital marketing, and data-driven decision-making roles.

Intended Learning Outcomes

The following intended learning outcomes are derived qualitatively from the programme aims and learning outcomes published on the Departmental page, and are organised under Knowledge, Skills, and Competences.

Knowledge

  • Demonstrate foundational knowledge across the core business disciplines — management, economics, financial and management accounting, finance, marketing, operations, and human resources.
  • Explain the principles of entrepreneurship, innovation, and new business model creation, and the processes for turning innovative ideas into viable ventures.
  • Understand how digital technologies (including cloud, blockchain, e-commerce, AI, and business analytics) reshape business models, processes, and value creation.
  • Recognise corporate objectives alongside contemporary social, ethical, and environmental responsibilities, including corporate governance and business ethics.

Skills

  • Develop strategic and operational business plans and design innovative solutions that address consumer needs efficiently.
  • Apply quantitative and analytical methods — mathematics, statistics, quantitative research methods, and business analytics — to business problems.
  • Market innovative products and services effectively, including through digital marketing, web analytics, and customer-journey approaches.
  • Communicate professionally in academic and business English, including presentations, reports, and correspondence.

Competences

  • Create startups and lead the digital transformation of existing businesses, leveraging new technologies for promising digital ventures.
  • Manage business operations competitively within changing domestic and international markets.
  • Work effectively in teams to address real social and business problems, exercising leadership, negotiation, and conflict-management skills.
  • Act with professional and ethical judgement, integrating social and environmental considerations into business decisions.

Programme Structure

The programme comprises 240 ECTS over four years (eight semesters). Specialisation courses are introduced from Year 2 (Semester 4) onward and intensify in Years 3 and 4. Students may take an internship (MGT 390) or a thesis (MGT 391) in place of certain electives, subject to a minimum GPA requirement.

Course Descriptions

English Language Courses

LCE 129 — English for Academic Purposes

4 ECTS

A three-hour weekly course providing academic English instruction at B1–B2 CEFR level. It uses authentic materials covering general topics (student profiles, language learning, intercultural communication), academic topics (study skills, learning modes, bibliography review), and field-specific topics (innovation, entrepreneurship, social entrepreneurship, investments, fundraising).

LCE 130 — English for Management

4 ECTS

A three-hour weekly course providing specialised academic English at B2 CEFR level. It covers management-specific topics such as leadership, business meetings, workplace technology, renewable energy management, and customer service, and teaches various text types and writing approaches.

LCE 207 — English for Business Communication

4 ECTS

A three-hour weekly course developing business communication English at B2–C1 CEFR level. It focuses on written and oral skills: public presentations, workplace communication, professional correspondence, email writing, memos, formal reports, and the role of technology in communication.

Core Management Courses

MGT 100 — Introduction to Management

6 ECTS

Covers basic management principles and functions, managerial roles, and how changes in the business environment affect decision-making. Analyses internal and external environmental factors.

MGT 101 — Economics I

6 ECTS

An introductory microeconomics course covering economic thinking, basic concepts, supply and demand mechanisms, market price determination, elasticity, market efficiency, and production costs under different market structures.

MGT 102 — Economics II

6 ECTS

An introduction to macroeconomic theory, addressing national income, inflation, unemployment, economic growth, productivity, financial systems, central banking, and open-economy concepts.

MGT 110 — Mathematical Methods in Economics and Management I

6 ECTS

Covers limits, continuity, derivatives, integrals, and optimisation theory, focusing on mathematical analysis applicable to economic and business problem-solving.

MGT 111 — Statistical Methods in Economics and Management I

6 ECTS

Introduces probability theory and statistical concepts for quantitative research in economics and business management.

MGT 140 — Principles of Financial Accounting

6 ECTS

Examines fundamental accounting principles, conventional measurement and reporting, double-entry bookkeeping, income statements, balance sheets, financial statement preparation, and ratios.

MGT 141 — Management Accounting

6 ECTS

Covers cost accounting, strategic pricing, budgeting, standard costing, decision-making, capital investment evaluation, working capital management, and responsibility accounting.

MGT 142 — Information Technology for Business

6 ECTS

Provides IT fundamentals for business applications, system development methods, MS-Excel proficiency, and an introduction to statistical packages (EViews, SPSS).

MGT 201 — Business Ethics

6 ECTS

A theoretical course examining ethics in business, including contemporary issues such as transparency, meritocracy, and ethical values, with case-study analysis.

MGT 202 — Corporate Law

6 ECTS

Provides knowledge of the Cypriot legal system, sources of law, court structure, interpretation principles, human rights, and company law affecting business operations.

MGT 203 — Commerce

6 ECTS

Examines international business dimensions through international business management, analysing complex problems arising from global expansion, including cultural, political, economic, and technological considerations.

MGT 220 — Introduction to Entrepreneurship

6 ECTS

Introduces entrepreneurship concepts, the characteristics of business owners, and the implementation stages required for creating new enterprises.

MGT 221 — Finance I

6 ECTS

Covers financial concepts and tools: financial institutions, markets, securities, loan capital models, interest rates, yield curves, the time value of money, SME financing, bond and stock valuation, investment risk measurement, the CAPM model, and capital investment evaluation.

MGT 222 — Finance II

6 ECTS

Introduces the basics of corporate financial management: working capital management, inventory, accounts management, investment evaluation techniques, and risk management strategies for interest-rate and currency-risk hedging.

MGT 230 — Introduction to Digital Transformation

6 ECTS

Develops understanding of how digital technologies drive business model changes, process transformation, value creation, and strategic development. Covers digital tools (blockchain, cloud technologies) and the characteristics of platform-dependent businesses.

MGT 241 — Operations Management

6 ECTS

Provides a broad understanding of operational management concepts: strategy, process design, inventory management, scheduling, and quality management, with business applications.

MGT 260 — Principles of Marketing

6 ECTS

Helps students understand marketing's contribution to business strategy and how marketing plans are developed, implemented, and evaluated.

MGT 302 — Negotiation Techniques and Conflict Management

6 ECTS

Teaches negotiation principles, strategy development, process management, and skills for handling difficult negotiations and personnel/customer conflicts.

MGT 350 — Human Resources Management

6 ECTS

Examines HR and development practices from a strategic perspective: departmental roles and functions, job analysis, staffing, training, evaluation, labour relations, compensation systems, and current challenges.

MGT 351 — Organizational Behavior

6 ECTS

Addresses organisational behaviour principles: theory, individual and group behaviour, motivation, leadership, group dynamics, organisational culture, decision-making, and change management.

MGT 361 — Consumer Behavior

6 ECTS

Analyses consumer behaviour principles and applications: purchase decision processes, motivation, personality, lifestyle, attitudes, market segmentation, and social and cultural context.

MGT 370 — E-Commerce

6 ECTS

Provides knowledge of e-commerce and its role in business: e-commerce strategy, electronic marketing, electronic information, related applications, and internet technologies.

MGT 380 — Quantitative Research Methods

6 ECTS

Introduces quantitative research methods and techniques: problem definition, data analysis, sampling, research design, descriptive and inferential statistics, regression models, and special topics such as factor analysis.

MGT 402 — Corporate Governance

6 ECTS

Familiarises students with corporate governance — the policies and procedures for organisational management, board functioning, relationships with managers, and the influence of various organisational stakeholders.

MGT 426 — Strategic Management

6 ECTS

Covers understanding, application, and evaluation of organisational strategy: appropriate strategy development, implementation, evaluation, and identification of success factors.

MGT 453 — Leadership Capabilities

6 ECTS

Addresses leadership theory, styles, and development in industry. Examines motivation, the relationship between management and leadership, change and crisis management, team dynamics, conflict resolution, business ethics, and emotional intelligence.

MGT 462 — Strategic Marketing

6 ECTS

Develops understanding of strategic marketing management across different environments. Students learn the organisational role of strategic marketing; how to analyse customers, competitors, the environment, and partners; and how to develop, implement, and regularly evaluate marketing plans.

Specialisation — Entrepreneurship and Innovation

MGT 321 — Innovation Management

6 ECTS

Familiarises students with innovation management concepts at strategic and operational levels. Covers idea evaluation, stakeholder engagement, and partnership creation for product implementation.

MGT 322 — Principles of New Business Models

6 ECTS

Introduces innovative business model concepts and their creation. Guides students through identifying structural elements, understanding the importance of innovation, and developing a methodology for new models.

MGT 323 — Principles of New Business Creation

6 ECTS

Teaches the knowledge needed to convert innovative ideas into businesses successfully: identifying and evaluating opportunities, developing ideas, conducting feasibility studies, and creating business plans.

MGT 324 — Finance and Entrepreneurship

6 ECTS

Covers basic financing sources, business valuation techniques, and financial management for startup companies, including venture capital, business angels, and startup financing processes.

MGT 425 — Development of Startups and SMEs

6 ECTS

Teaches entrepreneurial processes and business practices that lead to the development of existing businesses, emphasising startups and SMEs facing sustainability challenges. Covers identifying opportunities, creating business environments, and development strategies.

MGT 427 — International Entrepreneurship

6 ECTS

Examines companies that internationalise immediately after creation through the discovery and exploitation of opportunities in foreign markets — known as born globals or international new ventures.

MGT 428 — Social Entrepreneurship

6 ECTS

Teaches the basics of social design, innovation, and entrepreneurship through interdisciplinary collaborative learning. Students address real social problems in small teams and complete semester projects using crowdfunding.

Specialisation — Digital Enterprise

MGT 341 — Business Systems in the Digital Age

6 ECTS

Addresses the software and hardware requirements for digital-age management: cloud systems, security systems, e-commerce systems, and internet/wireless technologies.

MGT 342 — Consumer Behavior in the Digital Age

6 ECTS

Helps students understand how modern business success depends on managers' comprehension of digital-age consumer behaviour. Examines internal mental processes and digital marketing strategy applications using web analytics and customer-journey mapping.

MGT 343 — Basic Programming Principles

6 ECTS

Teaches fundamental programming-language principles and the learning of a higher-level language. Guides students through basic algorithm concepts, problem-solving via programming, object-oriented principles, and debugging.

MGT 344 — Digital Marketing and Analytics

6 ECTS

Examines the digital marketing shift in business approaches: viral marketing, search optimisation, social media, conversion rates, and mobile marketing technologies. Covers both theoretical and practical digital marketing knowledge.

MGT 445 — Artificial Intelligence and Business Analytics

6 ECTS

Introduces the application of AI and data analytics to business problems. Covers predictive analytics, supervised and unsupervised machine learning algorithms, data modelling, and risk management applications.

MGT 446 — Design and Digitization of Business Processes

6 ECTS

Examines the effects of digitisation on business operations: business model upgrades, operational model impacts, and process redesign requirements, emphasising the process-improvement opportunities created by new technologies.

MGT 447 — Digital Product

6 ECTS

Provides knowledge on developing digital products and services in line with modern consumer needs and technological trends. Covers the stages of the business process: identifying opportunities, idea evaluation, R&D, commercialisation, and business models.

How You Will Learn

The programme combines lectures, tutorials, project work, and applied assignments across the core, specialisation, and language courses. Quantitative and digital courses make use of software tools (for example MS-Excel, statistical packages such as EViews and SPSS, and programming and analytics environments), while entrepreneurship courses emphasise hands-on venture creation, business planning, and team-based projects.

Applied and Experiential Learning

  • Project-based learning in entrepreneurship and innovation courses, including new business creation, business modelling, and (in Social Entrepreneurship) real social problems addressed in small teams with crowdfunding-based projects.
  • Digital and analytics courses with practical work in programming, digital marketing and analytics, AI and business analytics, and business process digitisation.
  • An optional 400-hour internship (MGT 390) in real business environments, and an optional research thesis (MGT 391).

Language of Instruction

Courses are delivered primarily in Greek, with three dedicated English-language courses (LCE 129, LCE 130, LCE 207) developing academic and business English at B1–C1 CEFR levels.

Career Prospects

With the competencies and knowledge acquired, graduates can apply for positions in the private or public sector, domestically or internationally, across relevant fields. The programme prepares graduates for:

  • Strategic and operational roles in business management
  • Innovation and entrepreneurship positions, including founding and developing new ventures, startups, and SMEs
  • Digital transformation leadership roles
  • Marketing and financial management positions
  • International business roles

Close partnerships between the Department and the business world support internships and employment across most sectors of the Cypriot and global economy.
