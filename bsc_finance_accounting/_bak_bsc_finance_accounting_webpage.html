BSc in Finance and Accounting

Department of Finance, Accounting and Management Science · Faculty of Management and Economics · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The BSc in Finance and Accounting is a four-year undergraduate programme designed to provide graduates with the knowledge, professional acumen and skills required to distinguish themselves as professionals in the fields of finance and accounting. The programme combines a broad understanding of business with rigorous training in the analytical, quantitative and technological methods used to support strategic decision-making.

Students build a strong common foundation across the first two years — in economics, financial accounting, finance, management, mathematics, statistics, law and programming — before choosing a specialisation in either Finance or Accounting in their third year. Across the curriculum, graduates gain knowledge in financial accounting, corporate finance, financial management, investments, auditing, taxation and economics, and develop the critical thinking and IT skills needed to apply this knowledge to real strategic objectives.

Specialisations

1. Finance — Builds advanced expertise in corporate finance, investment management, financial econometrics, banking economics, derivatives, financial statement analysis and asset valuation. Depth electives extend into sustainable finance, fixed-income portfolio management, risk management, financial technology, alternative investments and behavioural finance. The track prepares graduates for careers in banking, investment, asset management, financial analysis and FinTech.

2. Accounting — Develops advanced competence in financial accounting, consolidated financial statements, tax accounting, auditing and corporate strategy and technology. Depth electives extend into contemporary issues in accounting, shipping accounting, oil and gas company accounting, advanced financial accounting, banking-sector accounting and accounting information systems. The track prepares graduates for careers in auditing, professional accountancy, taxation and financial reporting, and is aligned with leading professional accountancy qualifications (ICAEW, ACCA).

Both specialisations share a common third- and fourth-year structure that includes a supervised Practical Training placement (FAMS500, 12 ECTS) and a Dissertation (FAMS501, 12 ECTS).

Intended Learning Outcomes

The intended learning outcomes below are expressed in the Knowledge / Skills / Competences framework and are derived qualitatively from the programme aims and course content. They are illustrative and intended for confirmation by the Department.

Knowledge

  • Explain the core principles of microeconomics, macroeconomics and the functioning of financial systems and markets.
  • Describe the conceptual framework of financial accounting, the double-entry method, the accounting cycle and the preparation of financial statements under International Accounting Standards.
  • Outline theories and methods of corporate finance, financial management, investment management and asset valuation within a risk–return framework.
  • Identify the legal, regulatory, taxation, ethical and sustainability frameworks that govern business, finance and the accountancy profession.

Skills

  • Apply quantitative methods — calculus, statistics, regression analysis, econometrics and matrix computation — to economic, financial and accounting problems.
  • Use information technology and analytical tools (e.g. MS Excel, R, and data-analysis/visualisation software) to manage, analyse and interpret financial and accounting data.
  • Prepare, analyse and interpret financial statements and use this information for investment, financing and managerial decision-making.
  • Design and conduct independent research using appropriate methodologies, culminating in a supervised dissertation.

Competences

  • Exercise critical thinking and professional judgement to evaluate economic policy, business strategy and financial decisions.
  • Act ethically and responsibly, taking account of sustainability, governance and the public interest in finance and accounting practice.
  • Communicate financial and accounting information effectively and collaborate in professional team settings.
  • Operate independently and adapt to a changing professional environment, including continued study at postgraduate level.

Course Descriptions

Descriptions below are English translations of the official course descriptions published on the programme page. Course codes and ECTS are as published.

Common Core (Semesters 1–4)

FAMS111 — Principles of Microeconomics

6 ECTS

Microeconomics is a foundational pillar of economic science. This course presents elementary economic tools to enable students to understand and apply economic principles, develop economic thinking, and use basic tools to analyse markets and evaluate economic policies.

FAMS112 — Calculus and Applications

6 ECTS

This course familiarises students with fundamental concepts of limits, continuity, differentiation and integration. It introduces optimisation theory and equips students with appropriate mathematical methods for analysing economic, administrative and business problems using mathematical analysis.

FAMS113 — Principles of Financial Accounting

6 ECTS

The course introduces students to accounting, the need for financial information, and its reliability within regulatory frameworks. Objectives include understanding the conceptual framework of accounting (assets, liabilities, revenues, expenses), the double-entry method, the accounting cycle, and the preparation of financial statements.

LCE136 — English for Finance, Accounting and Management Science I

6 ECTS

FAMS114 — Personal Money and Capital Management

6 ECTS

This course provides tools for students to acquire financial and economic literacy. It covers concepts of economic and financial management needed to understand how various financial events affect decision-making, helping students develop rational attitudes toward sound financial choices throughout life.

FAMS121 — Principles of Macroeconomics

6 ECTS

This course provides a comprehensive introduction to macroeconomic theory and addresses economy-wide questions. Students develop an understanding of key macroeconomic concepts (national income, inflation, unemployment), the determinants of economic growth, the functioning of the financial system, and the basics of the international economy.

FAMS122 — Statistics

6 ECTS

The course introduces students to basic statistical concepts and analytical procedures used in quantitative research in economics and business administration. Students learn probability theory and statistical methods to correctly apply fundamental statistical techniques in analysis and interpretation.

FAMS123 — Principles of Finance

6 ECTS

This course helps students understand the fundamental principles of corporate financial management by combining theory with practice. It explains how financial theory applies to practical business problems and investment decisions, focusing on asset valuation within a risk–return framework.

LCE137 — English for Finance, Accounting and Management Science II

6 ECTS

[PLACEHOLDER — course description to be supplied]

FAMS124 — Principles of Management

6 ECTS

This course introduces students to general management principles through a historical review and various contemporary approaches. It covers the planning, organisation, direction and control functions, and examines the manager's role in promoting change and providing effective leadership.

FAMS211 — Financial Accounting I

6 ECTS

The course deepens knowledge of accounting for events affecting asset and liability elements and their valuation. Emphasis is placed on the valuation rules recommended by International Accounting Standards, developing the ability to use published accounting information for financial decision-making and analysis.

FAMS212 — Business Skills

6 ECTS

Designed to provide students with essential skills for business success through lectures, case studies and guest speakers. Students learn effective communication, critical thinking, collaborative work, information-technology applications, and data analysis using MS Excel for managing and analysing information.

FAMS213 — Commercial and Corporate Law

6 ECTS

This course presents Cyprus's legal system, and specifically the commercial law affecting companies and traders. It covers commercial transactions, commercial status, company formation and dissolution, corporate obligations, and the duties of directors and auditors.

FAMS214 — Statistics and Regression Analysis

6 ECTS

This course equips students with sufficient probability and statistical knowledge for advanced topics in their field. It covers moment-generating functions, random variables, covariance and correlation, the central limit theorem, estimation methods, distributions, hypothesis testing and regression models.

FAMS215 — Introduction to Programming

6 ECTS

This foundational course introduces programming concepts and computational thinking using the R language. Addressed to beginners, it covers data types, control structures, functions, and basic data manipulation and visualisation techniques, developing problem-solving skills through practical applications.

FAMS221 — International Financial Markets

6 ECTS

Examines the challenges faced by multinational corporations and international investors in global money markets. Covers the balance of payments, foreign exchange markets, international parity conditions, exchange rate theories and capital availability. Emphasises practical applications, with a focus on the EU and the euro's role in international transactions.

FAMS222 — Financial Management

6 ECTS

Introduces the fundamental elements of financial management. Provides comprehensive knowledge of the approaches used by companies and financial institutions to manage working capital, inventory and receivables, evaluate investments, and manage risk.

FAMS223 — Management Science

6 ECTS

Studies operational-research techniques and methodologies for optimal managerial decision-making. Emphasises problem-solving and model development in finance, management science, economics and the shipping sector using specialised software.

FAMS224 — Algebra and Matrix Computations

6 ECTS

Provides the mathematical and algorithmic knowledge for matrix problems essential in data science, multivariate statistics, econometrics and machine learning. Equips students to apply these techniques and develop critical thinking through MATLAB, R and Python labs.

FAMS225 — Management Accounting and Costing I

6 ECTS

Develops foundational costing concepts and teaches the use of accounting tools such as budgeting. Students learn to use accounting instruments to solve internal business-information problems and to prepare reports for managerial decision-making.

Finance Specialisation — Required Courses

FAMS310 — Financial Econometrics

6 ECTS

An advanced course providing adequate knowledge of financial econometrics. It covers economic and financial relationships, analyses significant empirical research results, and offers tools for applying econometric models to financial theory.

FAMS311 — Corporate Finance

6 ECTS

Develops an integrated understanding of corporate finance at both theoretical and practical levels. Uses case studies to introduce corporate financial management, reviews analytical tools, and explains their application to companies' economic problems.

FAMS312 — Investment Management

6 ECTS

Develops knowledge and understanding of asset allocation and portfolio composition and management. Enables students to critically interpret current theoretical and empirical research on portfolio theories and their implications for alternative portfolio compositions.

FAMS321 — Banking Economics

6 ECTS

Analyses monetary, banking and financial markets and institutions. Covers bond-market interest-rate determination, asymmetric-information problems, bank regulation, central banks, monetary-policy tools, and lessons from past crises.

FAMS322 — Research Methods

6 ECTS

Provides the training in research methodologies needed for students to conduct research in finance and accounting. Covers theoretical, applied and critical skills for effective research using tools from statistics, mathematics, econometrics and computer science.

FAMS323 — Derivatives

6 ECTS

Describes the characteristics and markets for options and forward contracts. Analyses value determinants and basic valuation techniques, and examines the role of derivatives in corporate finance and investment decisions, particularly for hedging financial risk.

FAMS411 — Financial Statement Analysis

6 ECTS

Develops the ability to use financial-statement information for investment, financing and managerial decision-making. Emphasises evaluating a company's financial position, including industry characteristics, competitive strategy, past performance and forecasting future results.

FAMS412 — Asset Valuation

6 ECTS

Teaches fundamental asset-valuation concepts and their practical application through case studies. Emphasises the practical use of financial tools for analysing real-world scenarios and for understanding valuation concepts and methodologies in depth.

Accounting Specialisation — Required Courses

FAMS324 — Financial Accounting III

6 ECTS

Develops students' knowledge and understanding of the application of International Accounting Standards in preparing consolidated financial statements for groups of companies.

FAMS325 — Tax Accounting I

6 ECTS

Explains Cyprus's tax system, principles and provisions, with examples of the various taxes imposed on companies, cooperatives, the self-employed and employees.

FAMS413 — Corporate Strategy and Technology

6 ECTS

Helps students understand how companies plan, develop and implement strategies using information technology and data.

Shared Final-Year Courses

FAMS429 — Sustainable Development — Sustainability and Ethics

6 ECTS

Develops knowledge of fundamental ethical challenges and enhances understanding of the diverse approaches that frame contemporary ethics and governance scandals. Cultivates the cognitive and emotional capabilities needed to address real-life business and professional accountant dilemmas.

FAMS500 — Practical Training (Internship)

12 ECTS

Provides students with the opportunity to understand problems and trends in the finance and accounting sectors within real working conditions. Allows students to assess the relevance of their academic studies and prepare for a successful entry into the labour market after graduation.

FAMS501 — Dissertation

12 ECTS

Requires students to complete independent research on a business topic under faculty supervision, using the knowledge and skills they have acquired. Develops advanced research skills, an understanding of research methodologies, and the ability to conduct sophisticated empirical and theoretical research applicable to business practice.

Finance — Depth Electives

FAMS316 — Sustainable Finance

6 ECTS

This course introduces students to green finance and how environmental parameters can be incorporated into financial decision-making. Students examine contemporary trends and the strategic business implications of social and environmental challenges.

FAMS326 — Fixed Income Portfolio Management

6 ECTS

Introduces the unique characteristics of fixed-income securities and reviews global debt markets. It covers bond-pricing calculations, yield interpretation, interest-rate risk measurement, and securitisation analysis including its various types and associated risks.

FAMS414 — Risk Management and Analysis

6 ECTS

Students gain a comprehensive understanding of financial risk and develop skills to measure and manage it. The course covers theoretical knowledge and practical techniques for analysing, evaluating and mitigating economic risks through various assessment methods.

FAMS415 — Financial Technology

6 ECTS

Familiarises students with rapidly developing technology in finance, covering blockchain, cryptocurrencies, advanced payment systems, big-data challenges and algorithmic trading. Students gain critical knowledge of FinTech fundamentals applicable to modern finance.

FAMS422 — Alternative Investments

6 ECTS

Provides fundamental knowledge of alternative investments including real estate, commodities, private equity and hedge funds. Students explore how these investments enable risk diversification and the pursuit of higher returns.

FAMS423 — Behavioural Finance

6 ECTS

Develops an understanding of the role of psychology in financial decision-making, explaining phenomena that classical finance cannot. Students critically examine research on the factors affecting investment decisions and capital markets.

Accounting — Depth Electives

FAMS318 — Contemporary Issues in Accounting

6 ECTS

Develops a deeper understanding of accounting in complex business environments and of accounting's role in relation to businesses, the environment and society. Emphasises controversial issues in measurement and reporting.

FAMS319 — Shipping Accounting

6 ECTS

Students specialise in financial accounting within the shipping industry, understanding the accounting particularities of shipping enterprises and developing the relevant conceptual knowledge.

FAMS328 — Oil and Gas Company Accounting

6 ECTS

Provides skills and knowledge about the nature, operations and reporting practices of petroleum and gas companies. Students learn to analyse, interpret and evaluate company performance.

FAMS424 — Auditing II

6 ECTS

Prepares students to interpret significant audit issues including the acceptance, planning, management, completion, conclusion and reporting processes for company audits.

FAMS425 — Tax Accounting II

6 ECTS

Students learn to calculate various taxes for individuals and legal entities, computing income tax, VAT and capital gains tax. The course emphasises ethical tax practices.

FAMS426 — Advanced Financial Accounting

6 ECTS

Prepares students to prepare financial statements for companies and corporate groups using International Accounting Standards and International Financial Reporting Standards.

FAMS427 — Financial Accounting in the Banking Sector

6 ECTS

Students develop expertise in addressing compliance and ethics issues in preparing financial reports and in auditing banking institutions.

FAMS428 — Accounting Information Systems

6 ECTS

Students learn to use computerised accounting programs to maintain accounting records electronically, produce statements such as balance sheets, and complete forms such as VAT returns.

Breadth Electives (available to both specialisations)

FAMS317 — Data Analysis for Finance and Accounting

6 ECTS

Students learn technological skills for using big data in decision-making, including data preparation, visualisation using Power BI, machine learning, and natural-language-processing techniques for extracting knowledge.

FAMS327 — Machine Learning for Finance and Accounting

6 ECTS

Teaches students to analyse different data types for knowledge extraction, recognising patterns and selecting appropriate methodologies and algorithms for various applications in finance and accounting.

How You Will Learn

The programme combines academic study with practical, technology-enabled and research-based learning. Teaching draws on lectures, case studies, guest speakers from industry, computer labs, and project work, with strong use of analytical software (e.g. MS Excel, R, Python, MATLAB and data-analysis/visualisation tools) across the curriculum.

Practical Training

A supervised Practical Training placement (FAMS500, 12 ECTS) gives students the opportunity to understand problems and trends in the finance and accounting sectors under real working conditions, assess the relevance of their academic studies, and prepare for entry into the labour market. Practical internship placements and summer internships are available in financial services firms.

Independent Research

In the final year, students complete a Dissertation (FAMS501, 12 ECTS) — an independent research project on a business topic conducted under faculty supervision, developing advanced research skills and methodological understanding.

Quantitative and Technological Skills

Across the four years, students build quantitative and computational competence through calculus, statistics, regression analysis, econometrics, matrix computation, programming (R), and electives in data analysis and machine learning for finance and accounting.

Professional Recognition and Career Prospects

Professional Recognition

The programme is aligned with leading professional accountancy qualifications and offers the following exemptions, as published on the programme page:

  • ICAEW (Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales): up to twelve (12) exemptions (subject to approval of the new professional-title structure).
  • ACCA (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants): nine (9) exemptions.

Typical Job Roles and Sectors

The programme provides excellent academic training and practical familiarity, opening direct employment opportunities in:

  • Audit firms
  • Banks
  • FinTech companies
  • The stock exchange and insurance companies
  • Policy organisations
  • Semi-public and public bodies

The programme also enables progression to postgraduate study, including Master's and doctoral programmes.

Employment Statistics

This programme admitted its first students in 2025. As a newly established degree it has not yet produced graduates, so graduate-employment statistics are not yet available. Graduate-outcome data will be reported as the first cohorts complete their studies.
