PhD in Hospitality and Tourism Management

Department of Tourism and Hospitality Management · School of Tourism Management, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The PhD in Hospitality and Tourism Management is the doctoral programme of the Department of Tourism and Hospitality Management at the Cyprus University of Technology. It is a research degree, awarded for an original and substantive contribution to knowledge in the study of tourism and hospitality. The programme was established in 2009 and renamed in 2022 from "PhD in Hotel and Tourism Management" to its current title.

Doctoral candidates undertake an extended, independent research project under the supervision of a faculty member, working at the frontier of a field that the Department regards as an economic, social and cultural activity central to development and quality of life. Tourism is a defining sector of the Cypriot economy, providing a rich applied context for doctoral research. Intended Learning Outcomes

The outcomes below are expressed qualitatively as the doctoral-level research competences the programme is designed to develop, derived from the Department's research aims. They are framed as Knowledge, Skills and Competences and should be read as indicative; formally approved learning outcomes are maintained by the Department.

Knowledge

  • Command of the theoretical foundations, key debates and current research frontiers of tourism and hospitality management at the level required to extend the field.
  • Critical understanding of advanced research design and of qualitative and quantitative methodologies as applied to tourism and hospitality phenomena.
  • Awareness of the social, environmental and economic dimensions and externalities of tourism, and of the policy and development context within which the sector operates.

Skills

  • Ability to formulate original, significant research questions and to design and execute a substantive, methodologically sound research project independently.
  • Ability to collect, analyse and interpret data using appropriate quantitative and qualitative techniques and research software.
  • Ability to communicate research findings to academic and professional audiences in writing and orally, including through peer-reviewed publication and conference presentation, in English.

Competences

  • Capacity to operate as an autonomous, ethically responsible researcher, managing a complex, long-term project to completion.
  • Capacity to make an original contribution to knowledge that withstands the scrutiny of expert peer review and a public defence.
  • Readiness to take on leadership and expert advisory roles in academia, research, policy and the tourism and hospitality industry.

Programme Structure

The PhD is a research degree carrying a total of 240 ECTS over a typical duration of five years, completed predominantly through independent supervised research culminating in a doctoral dissertation. The programme does not follow the taught-course-plus-thesis structure of a master's degree; the credit load reflects the full doctoral research undertaking rather than a fixed catalogue of taught courses.

Indicative Doctoral Milestones

The stages below describe the general shape of a doctoral degree and are provided for orientation only; the authoritative sequence, timing and credit allocation are set by the University's doctoral regulations and the Department.

  • Admission and supervision — appointment of a research advisor / supervisor and agreement of the research topic.
  • Research proposal — development and approval of a detailed proposal setting out research questions, literature review and methodology.
  • Comprehensive / qualifying examination — assessment confirming the candidate's readiness to proceed to full research (where required by regulation).
  • Research and dissertation — execution of the research programme and writing of the dissertation, with periodic progress review.
  • Dissertation defence — public defence of the completed dissertation before an examination committee.

Admission Requirements

Admission to the PhD is competitive and is normally organised around specific doctoral research topics advertised by the Department, each linked to a supervising faculty member. The requirements below combine the general expectations of the programme with the criteria published for recent doctoral positions; applicants should always check the current call for the topic they wish to pursue.

Eligibility

Applicants are normally expected to hold both a bachelor's degree and a master's-level qualification (MSc, MA or MBA) from a recognised university, in a field relevant to the chosen research topic — for example tourism management, hotel management, marketing, economics, political economy or a closely related discipline. Holders of qualifications deemed equivalent by the Cyprus Council for the Recognition of Higher Education Qualifications (KY.S.A.T.S.) are also considered.

Additional expectations published for recent doctoral positions include:

  • A strong academic record and demonstrated analytical and research aptitude.
  • Excellent / advanced English-language proficiency (with recognised certification where required), since the dissertation is completed and defended in English.
  • Computer literacy and, for research-intensive topics, skills in quantitative and/or qualitative research design and in analysis software such as SPSS, STATA, R or NVivo.
  • For some topics, documented research experience such as publications, conference presentations or participation in research programmes.
  • Full-time enrolment and commitment to the doctoral programme.

Required Application Materials

Recent doctoral calls required applicants to submit:

  • A motivation statement (approximately 500 words) explaining the applicant's research interests and career goals.
  • A research proposal (approximately 1,000 words) setting out the proposed research questions, a literature review and the intended methodology.
  • A detailed curriculum vitae (CV) highlighting the applicant's academic, professional and research background.

Applications and supporting documents are submitted online through the University's admissions portal, where applicants create an account, complete their profile and submit their programme preferences and documentation.

How You Will Learn

As a doctoral programme, the PhD is learned principally through supervised independent research rather than taught classes. The candidate's development is driven by a sustained research project, structured supervision and progressive milestones, with formal training provided where it supports the research.

Research Supervision

Each candidate works under the guidance of a research advisor / supervisor, who is a faculty member of the Department with expertise relevant to the research topic. Supervision covers the framing of research questions, research design, the conduct and analysis of the research, and the writing and defence of the dissertation. Recent doctoral positions were each tied to a named supervisor and a defined research theme.

Research Training

Candidates develop and apply advanced research methods — including quantitative and qualitative design and the use of analysis software (for example SPSS, STATA, R and NVivo) — to a standard appropriate to original doctoral research. Research training, methodology development and any required doctoral coursework support the candidate's progression toward an independent contribution to knowledge.

Recognition and Career Prospects

Professional Recognition

No professional-body exemptions apply to this doctoral programme. The PhD is an academic research degree; its value lies in the original contribution to knowledge and the advanced research competences it certifies, not in exemptions from professional qualifications.

Career Prospects

Doctoral graduates in hospitality and tourism management are equipped for careers that draw on advanced research, analytical and leadership skills. Typical destinations include, qualitatively:

  • Academia — university teaching and research posts, postdoctoral research and academic careers in tourism and hospitality management and related disciplines.
  • Research — research roles in institutes, think-tanks and organisations conducting tourism and hospitality research.
  • Policy and the public sector — advisory, analytical and policy roles in tourism authorities, development organisations and government bodies.
  • Industry leadership — senior, strategy and expert advisory roles within the hospitality, tourism, transport and wider services sectors.
