Overview

The PhD in Business Administration and Entrepreneurship is the doctoral programme of the Department of Management, Entrepreneurship and Digital Enterprise at Cyprus University of Technology. It is designed for outstanding graduates who wish to make an original contribution to knowledge through advanced, supervised research and to develop into independent scholars and thought leaders.

The department brings together distinguished academics and researchers with specialisations across the business sector — including management, innovation, entrepreneurship, marketing, and strategy — who hold academic qualifications and teaching experience from prominent universities in the USA, UK, Germany, and Australia. Doctoral candidates work closely with these faculty members to design and conduct research that addresses real-world challenges and advances their field.

The programme cultivates researchers who can become leaders in their field through research, combining rigorous theory with practical relevance, and preparing graduates for demanding careers in academia, research, and industry.

Intended Learning Outcomes

The intended learning outcomes below are expressed qualitatively as the research competences expected of a doctoral graduate, derived from the programme's stated aim of developing independent researchers and leaders in their field. They are grouped as Knowledge, Skills, and Competences.

Knowledge

  • A systematic and comprehensive understanding of a substantial body of knowledge at the forefront of their chosen field within management, entrepreneurship and innovation, or information systems.
  • Mastery of the theoretical frameworks, research paradigms, and scholarly debates relevant to their area of specialisation.
  • Detailed knowledge of advanced research methodologies — qualitative, quantitative, and mixed methods — and of the principles of research design and ethics.

Skills

  • The ability to conceive, design, and carry out an original programme of research that generates new knowledge and meets the standards of peer review.
  • The ability to critically analyse, evaluate, and synthesise complex information and competing perspectives, and to identify gaps that warrant investigation.
  • Advanced competence in collecting, analysing, and interpreting data using appropriate research methods and tools.
  • The ability to communicate research findings clearly and persuasively to specialist and non-specialist audiences, in writing and orally, including in academic publications and at conferences.

Competences

  • The capacity to work autonomously and with sustained scholarly integrity, managing a long-term research project to completion.
  • The judgement to make informed decisions in complex and unpredictable situations and to contribute to the advancement of the discipline and professional practice.
  • A commitment to ethical conduct, academic integrity, and the responsible application of research to address challenges in business and society.
  • The readiness to assume leadership roles in academic, research, or professional environments and to mentor and collaborate with others.

Programme Structure

The PhD is a research degree carrying a total of 240 ECTS and a typical full-time duration of four years. The doctorate is awarded on the basis of an original research thesis, defended in an oral examination, that makes a distinct contribution to knowledge in the candidate's field.

Stages of the Doctorate

The published programme page does not list specific taught course codes, ECTS allocations per stage, or a detailed stage-by-stage breakdown. The outline below describes the standard progression of a research doctorate; exact course codes, ECTS weights, and milestone requirements must be confirmed by the Department.

  • Coursework / research training — where required by the programme regulations, doctoral candidates complete advanced coursework and research-methods training in the early phase of study.
  • Comprehensive / qualifying examination — candidates demonstrate mastery of their field and readiness to proceed to independent research.
  • Research proposal — candidates develop and defend a detailed proposal setting out the research questions, methodology, and contribution.
  • Original research and thesis writing — the principal phase, conducted under the guidance of a supervisor and advisory committee.
  • Thesis submission and oral defence (viva) — public defence of the completed thesis before an examination committee.

Research Areas

The department's research is organised around three areas. Doctoral projects are developed within these areas in collaboration with faculty whose expertise spans management, innovation, entrepreneurship, marketing, and strategy.

The Three Research Areas

Management

Research in organisational management, strategy, leadership, human resource management, and the broader theory and practice of managing organisations. Doctoral projects in this area examine how organisations are structured, led, and adapted to deliver sustainable performance and positive impact.

Entrepreneurship and Innovation

Research into how new ventures are created and grown, how innovation is generated and managed, and how entrepreneurial activity contributes to economic and social value. This area connects to the department's mission of helping people create and manage pioneering and sustainable businesses with a positive impact on society.

Information Systems

Research on digital innovation, digital enterprise, and the role of information systems in transforming organisations and entrepreneurial activity — reflecting the department's focus on digital enterprise alongside management and entrepreneurship.

How You Will Learn

A doctorate is learned primarily through the practice of original research, undertaken with expert supervision rather than through taught classes alone. The programme combines individual supervision, research training, and integration into the department's research community.

Supervision

Each doctoral candidate works under the guidance of an academic supervisor (and, where applicable, an advisory committee) drawn from the department's faculty. Supervision involves regular meetings to plan and review the research, develop the candidate's methodological and writing skills, and prepare work for publication and examination.

[PLACEHOLDER — Department to supply: supervision model details — number of supervisors per candidate, advisory-committee composition, and progress-review schedule.]

Research Training

Candidates develop advanced competence in research design, methodology, data analysis, and academic writing. Where required by the programme regulations, this includes structured coursework and research-methods training in the early phase of the doctorate.

Research Community

Doctoral candidates are part of an active research community of distinguished academics and fellow researchers. They are encouraged to participate in seminars, present at conferences, and publish in peer-reviewed outlets, building the scholarly profile expected of an independent researcher.

Progression and Examination

Progress is assessed through milestones such as the research proposal, periodic progress reviews, and ultimately the submission and oral defence of the doctoral thesis before an examination committee.

Recognition and Career Prospects

Professional Recognition

No professional-body exemptions apply to this doctoral programme. The PhD is an academic research degree; its value lies in the original contribution to knowledge and the advanced research expertise it certifies, rather than in exemptions from professional examinations.

Career Prospects

Doctoral graduates are equipped to become leaders in their field through research and to pursue demanding careers across several sectors. Typical destinations include, qualitatively:

  • Academia — university teaching and research posts, postdoctoral research positions, and academic leadership.
  • Research institutes and think tanks — applied and policy-oriented research roles.
  • Industry, management, and consulting — senior analytical, strategic, and advisory roles in business, where advanced research and problem-solving skills are valued.

This doctoral programme was established in 2022 and has not yet produced graduates, so graduate-employment and destination statistics are not yet available. Outcome data will be reported as candidates complete and defend their dissertations.
