Bachelor in Hotel and Tourism Management

Department of Tourism and Hospitality Management · School of Tourism Management, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The Bachelor in Hotel and Tourism Management (Bachelor in Tourism and Hospitality Management) is a four-year undergraduate programme offered by the Department of Tourism and Hospitality Management at the Cyprus University of Technology. The programme provides specialised scientific training in the management of tourism and hotel enterprises, with emphasis on strategy, marketing, e-commerce, and human resource management.

The curriculum combines a strong foundation in general business education with core hotel and tourism management courses, supervised practical training in the industry, and a final-year thesis. In their final years, students choose one of two specialisation tracks — Hotel Management or Tourism Management — allowing them to tailor their studies toward their career goals while keeping a broad managerial base.

Specialisations

1. Hotel Management — Focused on the operational and managerial dimensions of hotel enterprises, covering service quality and total quality management, risk and crisis management in the hospitality industry, and expense (cost) management and control. Prepares graduates for management roles within hotel units and the wider hospitality sector.

2. Tourism Management — Focused on the planning, development, and marketing of tourism, covering tourist organisation and development, destination management and marketing, and cultural heritage tourism. Designed for students aiming for careers in tourism organisations, destination management, and the design of tourism experiences.

Intended Learning Outcomes

The following intended learning outcomes are derived qualitatively from the programme aims published by the Department. They are organised under Knowledge, Skills, and Competences. The Department should confirm or refine these against the official programme specification.

Knowledge

  • Explain the structure and operation of the hospitality and tourism industries, including hotel units, food and beverage operations, and tourism enterprises.
  • Describe the principles of general business education — economics, finance, accounting, marketing, and management — as they apply to tourism and hospitality.
  • Outline the role of public and private sectors in shaping tourism policy and in the planning and development of tourist destinations.
  • Identify the principles of sustainable tourism development, service quality, and total quality management.

Skills

  • Apply marketing, e-commerce, and revenue management techniques to hospitality and tourism enterprises.
  • Analyse business environments and use statistical and research methods to support decision-making.
  • Develop strategic and operational plans for hotel and tourism organisations.
  • Communicate effectively in professional English within the hospitality and tourism context.

Competences

  • Act as executive and managerial staff in the hotel and tourism industry, taking responsibility for strategic and operational decisions.
  • Manage human resources, risk, and quality in hospitality and tourism settings.
  • Integrate theory and supervised practical training to operate effectively in real working conditions.
  • Conduct independent research, culminating in a supervised thesis on a topic relevant to the chosen specialisation.

Programme Structure

The programme is delivered over four years (eight semesters) and requires a minimum of 240 ECTS. It comprises general business education and core hotel and tourism management courses, an English language programme, a six-month managerial internship, two specialisation tracks (chosen in the final years), elective courses, and a final-year thesis.

How You Will Learn

The programme combines academic coursework with applied learning. Across four years, students build a foundation in general business education and core hotel and tourism management, then specialise and complete an independent thesis. A central feature is the supervised managerial internship in the industry.

Practical Training

Students undertake a six-month managerial internship (HTM 390) in a department or unit related to their specialty. The University study guide describes the practical training as 1,200 hours of work-based experience, reinforcing theoretical knowledge and developing managerial skills in real working conditions. The internship requires completion of at least 110 ECTS beforehand.

Thesis

In the final years, students complete a supervised thesis (Thesis I and Thesis II) on a topic relevant to their specialisation, involving a literature review, data collection, analysis, and a public presentation.

English Language Programme

Students complete a sequence of three English courses (English for Academic Purposes; English for the Hospitality and Tourism Industry; English for Business Communication), progressing from B1–B2 to B2 level and developing academic and professional communication skills relevant to the industry.

Professional Recognition and Career Prospects

Professional Recognition

The programme holds certifications from global professional accountancy bodies, including the Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales (ICAEW) and the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA). Graduates may receive automatic exemptions where they meet the relevant pass thresholds (the source notes a 6-out-of-10 pass threshold).

Exact exemptions (which papers, and the precise conditions for ICAEW and ACCA) — to be confirmed and detailed by the Department.

Career Prospects

Graduates work as executive and managerial staff in the hotel and tourism industry. They develop strategic plans, manage operations, and design innovative travel experiences across tourism markets.

Typical roles and sectors include:

  • Hotel and resort management (front office, rooms division, food and beverage, operations)
  • Tourism organisations and destination management organisations
  • Travel, events, conference, and exhibition management
  • Marketing, e-commerce, and revenue management within hospitality and tourism enterprises
  • Human resources, quality, and risk management functions in the sector
