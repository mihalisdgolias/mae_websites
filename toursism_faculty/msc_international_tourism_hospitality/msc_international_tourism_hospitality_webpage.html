MSc in International Tourism and Hospitality Management

Department of Tourism, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship · School of Tourism Management, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The MSc in International Tourism and Hospitality Management is a postgraduate programme of the Cyprus University of Technology, offered by the Department of Tourism, Hospitality and Entrepreneurship at the Limassol campus. As its title indicates, the programme is concerned with the management of tourism on an international scale and with the operation of hospitality businesses, bringing together the strategic, organisational and commercial dimensions of these closely related sectors.

The programme is intended for graduates who wish to deepen their understanding of the international tourism and hospitality industries and to prepare for management and specialist roles within them. It addresses tourism as a global activity — spanning destinations, markets and the businesses that serve travellers — alongside the management of hospitality enterprises.

Knowledge

On the basis of the programme title, a graduate would be expected to demonstrate:

  • Understanding of the structure and dynamics of the international tourism industry, including destinations, markets and travel flows.
  • Knowledge of the principles and practices of hospitality business management.
  • Awareness of the economic, social and environmental context in which tourism and hospitality enterprises operate internationally.
  • Familiarity with management theory as applied to service-based and experience-based industries.

Skills

  • Apply management concepts and analytical methods to problems in tourism and hospitality organisations.
  • Evaluate strategic and operational decisions within an international and cross-cultural setting.
  • Interpret market and industry information to support planning and decision-making.
  • Communicate analysis and recommendations clearly to professional audiences.

Competences

  • Take responsibility for managing functions or projects within tourism and hospitality enterprises.
  • Work effectively in diverse, international teams and service environments.
  • Adapt to change and exercise informed, ethical professional judgement.
  • Pursue continued professional learning within the tourism and hospitality sectors.
