BSc in Data Science

Department of Finance, Accounting and Management Science · Faculty of Management and Economics · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

The BSc in Data Science provides a comprehensive foundation in statistical, mathematical and computational methods for advanced data analysis. The programme is designed to meet the growing global demand for data science professionals and quantitative analysts, equipping students with modern techniques in data mining, data management and analysis, machine learning and computational statistics.

Across four years of study, students build strength in mathematics and statistics, in programming and computational tools, and in the applied analytical methods that turn data into evidence and decisions. The curriculum combines a rigorous quantitative core with a wide selection of elective courses, allowing students to broaden their expertise in areas such as data mining, network science, information retrieval, the Internet of Things, and natural language processing.

Intended Learning Outcomes

The following intended learning outcomes are expressed qualitatively from the programme aims and the body of courses offered. They are organised under Knowledge, Skills, and Competences and should be reviewed and formally approved by the Department.

Knowledge

On successful completion of the programme, graduates are expected to:

  • Understand the core mathematical and statistical foundations of data science, including calculus, linear algebra and matrix computation, probability, inferential statistics and regression.
  • Explain the principles of computer programming and modern data-science tools, including object-oriented programming, SQL, Python, R and version control.
  • Describe the main concepts, techniques and applications of artificial intelligence, machine learning and statistical (multivariate and Bayesian) learning.
  • Understand methods for data management, database design, data and text mining, and data visualisation.
  • Recognise the role of optimisation, econometrics, graph/network theory and game theory in analysing complex quantitative problems.

Skills

On successful completion of the programme, graduates are expected to be able to:

  • Design, implement and document programs and data pipelines using contemporary languages and tools.
  • Apply statistical and machine-learning methods to model data, evaluate results, and interpret them critically.
  • Build and query databases and process large, unstructured datasets, including text.
  • Formulate and solve optimisation and decision-making problems relevant to business, economics and management.
  • Communicate analytical results effectively through visualisation and technical writing, including in English for a professional data-science audience.

Competences

On successful completion of the programme, graduates are expected to:

  • Combine technical and business skills to solve modern, real-world problems through data analysis.
  • Work independently on an extended research project (dissertation) and conduct enquiry at an advanced level.
  • Apply data-science methods responsibly, with awareness of ethical considerations in analysis and artificial intelligence.
  • Adapt to emerging techniques and tools and continue learning in a rapidly evolving field.
  • Integrate into the professional labour market and contribute to analytical and quantitative roles across sectors.

Course Descriptions

Year 1 — Core Courses

DSC_110 — Calculus and Applications

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course aims to familiarise students with the basic concepts of limit, continuity, differentiation and integration. It also introduces optimisation theory and mathematical methods for analysing economic and business problems.

DSC_111 — Probability and Statistics

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course covers the foundations of probability and statistics, including probability distributions, random variables, covariance and correlation, the central limit theorem, methods of estimation, and hypothesis testing.

DSC_112 — Introduction to Programming

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course aims to familiarise students with the main principles of computer programming, using Java. It covers object-oriented principles and good code design with reusable libraries.

DSC_113 — Programming for Data Science

[ECTS — to be supplied]

On completion, students acquire knowledge of the basic programming principles for working with databases, develop data-handling capabilities, apply computational thinking, become familiar with modern tools such as SQL, Python, R and Git, and understand the data-analysis process.

LCE_138 — English for Data Science I

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course enables students to communicate at the B1–B2 CEFR level. It is specifically designed for the needs of students in the data-science field, focusing on technical writing and specialised vocabulary for data-science professionals.

DSC_120 — Algebra and Matrix Computations

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Matrix analysis and computation are widely used across many areas of data science, including multivariate statistics, machine learning and optimisation. The course provides the mathematical and algorithmic tools for matrix problems that are essential to advanced topics.

DSC_121 — Inferential Statistics and Regression Analysis

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Students acquire the probability and statistics knowledge necessary for advanced topics. The course covers moment-generating functions, random variables, correlation, the central limit theorem, estimation, maximum likelihood, distributions, and hypothesis testing.

DSC_122 — Advanced Programming Topics

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Students extend their programming knowledge using object-oriented Java and gain experience designing and programming integrated systems. The course emphasises technologies for database access and the creation of graphical user interfaces.

DSC_123 — Introduction to Statistics for Data Science

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course aims to provide basic statistical knowledge using a statistical software package, giving students the foundational concepts needed for further applications of statistics.

LCE_139 — English for Data Science II

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course develops B2-level communication ability and focuses specifically on the complex and evolving aspects of data science, exploring advanced statistical models, machine learning, natural language processing, and the ethics of artificial intelligence through lectures, projects and case studies.

Year 2 — Core Courses

DSC_210 — Computational Optimization

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The primary objective is to learn the fundamental theory of computational optimisation, numerical algorithms and their applications. The course covers linear programming (the simplex method and duality), unconstrained and constrained optimisation, Lagrange multipliers, and the KKT conditions, with an emphasis on applications.

DSC_211 — Introduction to Econometrics

[ECTS — to be supplied]

This introductory econometrics course aims for students to acquire the statistical and econometric knowledge necessary to understand, evaluate and interpret econometric research using basic regression models, and to examine violations of model assumptions.

DSC_212 — Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course offers a comprehensive introduction to the fundamental concepts, techniques and applications of artificial intelligence and machine learning. Students explore a range of algorithms, neural networks, deep learning, reinforcement learning, and real-world applications.

DSC_213 — Graph Theory and Networks

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course introduces graph theory and discusses its applications in computer science, with an emphasis on reading, understanding and developing graph-theoretic proofs. Topics include degrees, paths, trees, cycles, Eulerian graphs, bipartite graphs, matching, connectivity, network flows, colouring, and Hamiltonian cycles.

DSC_220 — Management Science

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course studies operational-research techniques and methodology and how to apply them to optimal managerial decision-making. The emphasis is on problem-solving and the study of models in finance, management science, economics and the shipping sector.

DSC_221 — Game Theory

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course presents and analyses the basic tools of game theory so that students understand its significance for decision-making. It examines equilibrium concepts in static and dynamic games under conditions of complete/perfect and incomplete information.

DSC_223 — Data Visualization

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course introduces students to the field of data visualisation, providing the basic knowledge needed to visualise data and models. The primary objective is to understand the conversion of data into visual form in order to describe and explore data, test hypotheses, and present evidence.

Year 3 — Core Courses

DSC_311 — Bayesian Modeling

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The primary objective is to equip students with the skills to perform and interpret Bayesian data analyses. The course offers a thorough introduction to the Bayesian approach to data analysis and modelling, together with an introduction to the computational tools that make the practical use of Bayesian methods possible.

DSC_312 — Multivariate Methods and Statistical Learning

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Multivariate statistical models and methods are essential for analysing complex, structured and possibly high-dimensional data across the sciences and industry. The course familiarises students with the methodology of multivariate statistics and statistical learning, with practical application in the R language.

DSC_313 — Databases

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course equips students with the knowledge required to design databases and database systems and to implement them using the SQL language.

DSC_320 — Data and Text Mining

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course teaches students to extract useful information from large, unstructured text datasets. It includes text-data preprocessing, representation suitable for algorithms, and the application of techniques such as classification, clustering and association-rule mining, as well as sentiment analysis, topic modelling, web scraping, and social-media mining.

DSC_332 — Statistical Machine Learning

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Statistical machine learning is an increasingly important approach for extracting valuable information from data. Used appropriately, it enables data-driven problem-solving for issues that cannot be solved through theory alone. The course develops the theoretical and practical fundamentals of machine-learning problems and the commonly used solutions.

Year 4 — Core Courses, Dissertation & Internship

DSC_410 — Computational Statistics and Econometrics

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course studies the linear regression models used in statistics and econometrics, which are important for the effective application of economic theory to real economic problems. It is intended for students who wish to deepen their knowledge of computational statistics and econometrics, covering computational methods for solving statistics and econometrics problems.

DSC_411 — Advanced Topics in Data Science

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course covers modern computational and analytical techniques, including: Natural Language Processing (the strengths and weaknesses of NLP technology and analytical techniques such as stylometry and topic modelling); Deep Learning (reinforcement learning, variational autoencoders for data generation, and meta-learning); the foundations of Large Language Models (chain-of-thought prompting and GPT architecture); Scientific and Statistical Computing (non-linear optimisation and optimality conditions); MapReduce programming (scalable data processing with Hadoop and Spark); and streaming algorithms and coding theory (data compression, error detection, and real-time data-stream processing).

DSC_421 — Advanced Topics in Statistical Data Science

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Designed for students with a strong foundation in statistical data analysis who wish to enhance their skills, the course covers high-dimensional data analysis, large-scale data analysis, combinatorial statistical methods, optimisation, heuristic statistical methods, econometrics, statistical optimisation methods (e.g. EM algorithms), stochastic optimisation and bootstrapping, data simulation, ridge regression, complex data structures, and non-normal data. Through a combination of lectures and workshops, students develop a deep understanding of advanced statistical data-science techniques and the ability to solve real problems.

DSC_422 — Advanced Topics in Statistics

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course addresses students with a reasonably good foundation in statistics who wish to further enhance their skills in statistical science. Through guided exploration of advanced statistical learning methods and supporting analysis tools, students develop a broad understanding of the advantages and disadvantages of choosing particular methods for specific problem situations.

DSC_400 — Internship / Practical Training

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The basic objective of the Practical Training is to give students the opportunity to understand the problems and trends of data science by placing them in real working environments. Students assess the relevance of the field to their academic studies and prepare for successful integration into the labour market after graduation.

DSC_450 — Dissertation

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The student completes a dissertation under the supervision of a department academic. The student is expected to carry out independent research on an approved topic using the knowledge and skills developed during the programme. The course aims to provide the training required to conduct advanced research, to develop an understanding of alternative epistemological positions and of qualitative and quantitative approaches, to develop the study skills needed to manage a research project, to offer opportunities to engage with cutting-edge research, and to enable students to conduct research at an advanced level applicable to business and the labour market.

Delivered across two parts (Part A in Semester 7 and Part B in Semester 8).

Elective Courses

DSC_440 — Advanced Topics in Linear Modeling and Classification

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course provides students with an overview of modern linear regression and classification methods and the skills to use the related R packages for the analysis of simple datasets.

DSC_351 — Linear and Generalized Linear Models

[ECTS — to be supplied]

The course provides knowledge of the definition of linear models and the theoretical least-squares approach to estimation, using QR decomposition for statistical inference. It also covers generalised linear models and the theoretical treatment of maximum-likelihood estimation, together with model-fitting procedures, model diagnosis, stepwise model construction, and the interpretation of results using R functions.

DSC_350 — Stochastic Processes

[ECTS — to be supplied]

Stochastic processes are measurable phenomena that develop randomly over time. The course covers Markov chains (using conditional probability for flexible random processes), random walks (which serve as fundamental building blocks), and renewal theory (the study of processes that "start over"). Such processes are common tools in economics, biology, psychology and operational research.

How You Will Learn

The programme combines theoretical foundations with extensive hands-on, computational practice. Students learn through lectures, laboratory and software-based work, projects and case studies, an optional internship, and an individual dissertation in the final year.

Tools and Practice

Practical work draws on contemporary data-science tools and languages used throughout the curriculum, including SQL, Python, R, Java and Git, and on statistical software packages for applied statistics and machine learning.

Project and Research Work

Beyond coursework, students undertake an individual dissertation (DSC_450), carried out across the final year under academic supervision, and may complete an optional internship / practical training (DSC_400) in a real working environment.

Language Support

Dedicated "English for Data Science" courses (LCE_138 and LCE_139) develop the technical writing and specialised vocabulary that data-science professionals need, targeting B1–B2 and B2 CEFR levels.

Career Prospects

The programme prepares graduates for the strong and growing demand for data-science professionals and quantitative analysts. It emphasises a combination of technical and business skills, high earning prospects, and the ability to solve modern, real-world problems through data analysis.

Typical job roles for graduates include:

  • Data analyst
  • Data engineer
  • Business intelligence analyst
  • Machine learning engineer
  • Database administrator
  • Market research analyst

Graduates are also suited to support roles in applied analytical domains such as epidemiology and climate-change monitoring, as well as quantitative and analytical positions across the finance, business, technology and public sectors.
