BSc in Commerce, Finance and Shipping

Department of Finance, Accounting and Management Science · Faculty of Management and Economics · Cyprus University of Technology

Overview

Programme status: This undergraduate programme is being phased out. The last intake of new students was in the 2024–25 academic year. Students admitted in 2024–25 complete their studies in the Finance specialisation only. No new admissions are taking place.

The BSc in Commerce, Finance and Shipping (Πτυχίο Εμπορίου, Χρηματοοικονομικών και Ναυτιλίας) is a four-year undergraduate degree offered by the Department of Finance, Accounting and Management Science within the Faculty of Management and Economics. The programme is designed to equip graduates with the knowledge and capabilities required for professional positions in financial institutions, auditing firms, investment companies, and business service organisations.

The programme builds a strong foundation in economics, mathematics, statistics, accounting, and commerce during the first two years, common to all students, and then develops advanced specialist expertise in finance during the final two years. It aims to develop students' ability to analyse, understand and evaluate issues concerning the operation of financial markets, and to understand the fundamental principles of financial operations within modern economic systems.

Intended Learning Outcomes

The published programme materials present learning objectives at the level of individual courses rather than as an integrated, programme-wide statement. The outcomes below are derived qualitatively from the programme's stated aims and course objectives; the Department should review and confirm them as the official programme-level learning outcomes.

Knowledge

  • Understand the fundamental principles of financial operations within modern economic systems.
  • Explain core concepts in micro- and macroeconomics, international economics, and the operation of financial markets.
  • Describe the principles of financial and management accounting and the analysis of financial statements.
  • Understand the principles of corporate finance, banking, investment management, financial derivatives, and the financing of shipping.

Skills

  • Analyse, understand and evaluate issues concerning the operation of financial markets.
  • Apply mathematical, statistical, and econometric methods to economic and business problems.
  • Use financial-statement information to support investment, financing, and management decisions.
  • Apply quantitative and qualitative research methods to problems in finance.

Competences

  • Take on professional positions in financial institutions, auditing firms, investment companies, and business service organisations.
  • Operate effectively in real working environments, as developed through the internship.
  • Pursue further study at master's level and professional accountancy qualifications (e.g. ACA, ACCA).
  • Work towards leadership roles in the private or public sector, or as self-employed entrepreneurs.

How You Will Learn

The programme combines lectures, tutorials, and quantitative and applied work across economics, mathematics, statistics, econometrics, accounting, and finance. Specialist finance courses build progressively on the common foundation of Years 1–2, and the curriculum integrates professional terminology and communication in English through the English-for-Finance language courses.

Applied and Research Components

  • Internship (CFS 441, 12 ECTS): a structured placement immersing students in real working environments in the finance and shipping industries, helping them evaluate the relevance of their studies and prepare for entry into their chosen sector.
  • Research Methods in Finance (CFS 420): develops the research skills needed for independent study in finance.
  • Dissertation (CFS 440, 12 ECTS, elective): an independent research project for students who choose the research route.

Language of Instruction

Most courses are taught in Greek. The English-for-Finance language courses (LCE 107, LCE 108, LCE 410) are delivered in English, and professional terminology in English is emphasised throughout the programme.

Professional Recognition and Career Prospects

Professional Recognition and Exemptions

The programme holds professional-body recognition that entitles graduates to exemptions from professional accountancy qualifications.

ICAEW — Chartered Accountant (ACA)

Graduates automatically receive exemption from eight modules of the ICAEW ACA qualification. The detailed exemption matrix is published by ICAEW (search by institution ID 492 in the ICAEW academic-credits directory).

ACCA — Association of Chartered Certified Accountants

Undergraduate students are entitled to ACCA exemptions; the exact number varies by year of graduation. ACCA provides an automatic exemptions-calculator tool for confirmation.

Career Prospects

The degree is designed to support immediate employment in management and economics roles. Graduates pursue careers in:

  • Financial institutions and banking
  • Auditing firms
  • Investment companies
  • Business service organisations
  • Organisations involved in formulating economic policy

The programme also prepares graduates for further study, including master's programmes at recognised universities and professional qualification programmes such as ACA and ACCA. With advanced specialisation, graduates can progress towards leadership positions in the private or public sector, or work as self-employed entrepreneurs.
